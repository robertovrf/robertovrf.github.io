About me

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Computer Networks Laboratory (LRC) at the State University of Campinas (Unicamp), Brazil. I research self-adaptive and autonomic computing techniques for service placement and resource allocation in the edge-cloud continuum to support video streaming applications. Prior to that, I worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Federal University of Goiás, Brazil (2020-2022), where I studied Autonomous Composition of Software for Smart Cities. I was also a Research Associate at Lancaster University, UK (2017-2020), where I conducted research on Distributed Emergent Systems for Data Centers. During my time at Lancaster as a Researcher, I spent the summer of 2019 as a Visiting Researcher at IRISA/INRIA Université de Rennes 1, in France. I obtained a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Lancaster University in 2018, and I hold an M.Sc. and a B.Sc. degree in Computer Science from the Federal University of Goiás (2013).

Research interests

I am interested in developing large-scale systems that can self-design at runtime to meet their user-defined goals in situations where their executing environment continually changes. Achieving this vision mainly entails abstracting many aspects of a system's design into a simple action/reward matrix to be used by reinforcement learning algorithms during the system's lifetime. I have applied this concept to create the very first self-designing web-serving infrastructure that can autonomously optimise their performance when subjected to varying workloads. Self-designing systems actively experiment with different ways in which to assemble their internal components to maximise the satisfaction of their goals. Further to that, I have been currently investigating how to apply the same concept to microservices, IoT systems and web cache applications.
